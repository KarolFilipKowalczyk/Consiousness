\section{What the Theory Is and Is Not}

\subsection{What the Theory IS}

\begin{itemize}
\item A mathematical framework with entropic scaling avoiding exponential requirements
\item A unification of major consciousness theories under realistic constraints
\item A source of testable predictions about neural and behavioral signatures
\item An explanation of temporal phenomenology through computational collapse
\item A dissolution (not solution) of the hard problem via identity claim
\item A framework compatible with both classical and quantum substrates
\end{itemize}

\subsection{What the Theory is NOT}

\begin{itemize}
\item A claim that brains are literally finite-state machines
\item A reduction of consciousness to computation alone
\item A denial of biological or physical constraints
\item A solution that requires exponential resources
\item A purely philosophical speculation without empirical content
\item A claim that current AI systems are conscious
\end{itemize}
